Welcome!

Hello and welcome! My name is Maria Garcia Iglesias, and I'm so happy you're here. I teach Spanish to K-4 students at Hawthorne Elementary School in Teaneck, New Jersey, and every day I feel lucky to share my language and culture with young learners.

I was born and raised in Spain, surrounded by stories, songs, and traditions that shaped who I am today. From an early age, I loved the way language connects people, how a song can make you feel at home, or how a story can bring a whole culture to life. That love eventually led me to pursue my degree in Education and to begin a new adventure in the United States, where my journey as a Spanish teacher truly took shape.

Wanting to grow as both a teacher and a learner, I enrolled in the Master of Arts in the Teaching of Languages (MATL) program at The University of Southern Mississippi, with an emphasis in Spanish. I'll be completing my degree in December 2025, and this experience has been incredibly meaningful. It has deepened not only my knowledge of language teaching, but also my passion for inspiring students to explore the world through Spanish.

In my classroom, I love creating moments that feel alive, whether we're dancing to a traditional song, acting out a folktale, or discovering new places on the map. I want my students to experience language not just through words, but through music, movement, culture, and imagination.

I'm always eager to learn new ways to make my lessons engaging, so I often attend webinars, workshops, and conferences, and whenever possible, I travel. Each trip gives me fresh stories, ideas, and perspectives that I bring back to my students.

Above all, my goal is simple: I want every child who walks into my classroom to feel curious, confident, and excited to learn Spanish. Teaching is more than my profession, it's my passion and a part of who I am.
