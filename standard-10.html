Standard 10: Leadership and Collaboration

Standard Definition

The teacher seeks appropriate leadership roles and opportunities to take responsibility for student learning, to collaborate with learners, families, colleagues, other school professionals, and community members to ensure learner growth, and to advance the profession.

Rationale

This artifact is a strong representation of InTASC Standard 10: Leadership and Collaboration, because it demonstrates how I use scholarly inquiry, policy analysis, and reflective practice to engage critically with issues that shape the educational experiences of multilingual learners in the United States. Standard 10 emphasizes a teacher's responsibility to work collaboratively with colleagues, families, communities, and policymakers to advance equitable educational opportunities. Through this assignment, I developed a deeper understanding of the historical, political, and ideological forces that impact the families and communities I serve, preparing me to take an informed leadership role as a bilingual educator.

Across the articles and documentaries I read, from James Crawford's analysis of bilingual education policy to Lawton's critique of the "English Only" movement and Leeman's exploration of monolingual ideologies, I examined the powerful ways in which language policies influence classrooms, access to services, and students' sense of belonging. By synthesizing these perspectives, I developed the ability to communicate clearly about the impact of political decisions on multilingual communities. This positions me to collaborate more effectively with administrators, community members, and families when advocating for inclusive and linguistically responsive practices.

This assignment also strengthened my capacity to analyze public discourse and recognize how misleading narratives, xenophobia, and misinformation shape attitudes toward English learners. Understanding these dynamics is essential for a teacher because it allows me to challenge harmful assumptions and engage colleagues in informed discussions grounded in research rather than misconceptions. Through these reflections, I became better prepared to facilitate conversations at my school around bilingual education, language rights, and culturally sustaining practices.

Also, the documentaries, A Day Without a Mexican, El sue침o se vuelve realidad, and Los mexicanos me dieron trabajo y salvaron este pueblo, illuminate the lived experiences of immigrant families, an issue profoundly relevant in today's political climate, and their essential contributions to local communities. Incorporating these perspectives into my reflection reinforces my commitment to working with families as partners, aligning with Standard 10's call for collaboration that supports learners' growth and well-being. These insights encourage me to advocate for family engagement practices that honor the cultural and linguistic resources immigrant communities bring to our schools.

Finally, this artifact demonstrates my ability to critically examine complex social issues, communicate their implications clearly, and use this understanding to collaborate meaningfully with others. By engaging deeply with research and community perspectives, I cultivate the leadership qualities necessary to foster equitable, linguistically inclusive learning environments, making this assignment a strong representation of InTASC Standard 10.

Rationale

This reflection entry is a strong representation of InTASC Standard 10 because it demonstrates my ability to engage in leadership, collaboration, community interaction, and ongoing professional responsibility. In this journal entry, I show these dispositions through my persistence, outreach, communication efforts, and problem-solving mindset as I navigate the challenges of securing a service-learning placement.

First, through this entry, I wanted to illustrate my ability to initiate and sustain professional collaboration, which is a core element of Standard 10. Rather than waiting passively for opportunities, I took proactive steps by contacting multiple community organizations, including the Latin American Institute, the local library, the Boys & Girls Club, and local officials. This demonstrates my commitment to building partnerships that support learners and families, especially within the Hispanic community I serve. My continued communication with Dr. Bernal and other stakeholders shows my role as a collaborator who seeks mutually beneficial solutions to community needs.

Second, the reflection highlights my capacity to problem-solve and persist despite obstacles. Even when meetings were canceled or delayed, I did not withdraw. Instead, I expanded my outreach, adjusted my plans, and continue to follow up with professionalism. InTASC Standard 10 emphasizes a teacher's responsibility to "take initiative" and "work productively with school colleagues, families, and community members." My willingness to adapt, reschedule, seek alternatives, and maintain forward momentum illustrates this leadership stance and models resilience, an essential characteristic of reflective and responsible teaching.

Third, the entry reflects my ethical responsibility to serve diverse learners and families, especially those from historically underrepresented groups. By intentionally seeking organizations that support Hispanic families, I demonstrate an awareness of the cultural and linguistic needs of my school community. This aligns with Standard 10's expectation that teachers advocate for learners and engage with community resources to support student well-being. My determination to remain engaged, even when discouraged, underscores my belief in equitable access and meaningful community partnership.

Finally, this reflection demonstrates my professional self-awareness, an important aspect of Standard 10. I do acknowledge moments of frustration while reaffirming my responsibility to persevere. This reflective stance is consistent with the expectation that effective teachers continually evaluate their efforts, refine their strategies, and take responsibility for contributing positively to the learning community.

For these reasons, this service-learning reflection is an excellent representation of InTASC Standard 10: Leadership and Collaboration, showcasing my ongoing commitment to community engagement, problem-solving, and advocacy for the diverse families I serve.
