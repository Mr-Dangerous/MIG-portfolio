Standard 2: Learning Differences

Standard Definition

The teacher uses understanding of individual differences and diverse cultures and communities to ensure inclusive learning environments that enable each learner to meet high standards.

Artifact 1: Simulating Culture Shock-Confusion Activity

Course: WL 665 Sociocultural and Sociolinguistic (Spring 2024)

Rationale

I chose this artifact because it captures a moment of real cultural and emotional insight. For this activity, I attended a Polish Catholic Palm Sunday service, fully aware that I would be stepping into a space where I didn't understand the language or the customs. As soon as I walked in, I felt both curious and uneasy, surrounded by rituals and conversations that, even though they were familiar to me, the language created a barrier that made me uncomfortable. And while the setting was beautiful and spiritually moving, I couldn't escape the feeling of being an outsider.

This experience reminded me powerfully of what many of my students go through when they enter a new linguistic and cultural environment. I felt the confusion, isolation, and vulnerability that language learners often experience. It brought back memories of my arrival in the United States, when I didn't speak English and quite often felt lost. By putting myself intentionally in that position again, I gained a deeper understanding of the kinds of emotional and cognitive challenges language learners face every day.

This activity helped me reflect on how important it is to create a welcoming and inclusive classroom, where all students feel safe to take risks, make mistakes, and be part of a community, even if they're still finding their footing linguistically and culturally. It reinforced the idea that learning a language isn't just about vocabulary and grammar; it's about navigating identity, community, and belonging.

By experiencing culture shock firsthand, I strengthened my empathy and ability to design learning environments that are culturally responsive and supportive of all learners, exactly what the InTASC Standard 2 emphasizes.
