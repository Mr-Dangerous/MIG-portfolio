Standard 7: Planning for Instruction

Standard Definition

The teacher plans instruction that supports every student in meeting rigorous learning goals by drawing upon knowledge of content areas, curriculum, cross-disciplinary skills, and pedagogy, as well as knowledge of learners and the community context.

Artifact #1: WL665 Imperatives for Studying Language and Intercultural Communication in Today's Globalized World

Course: WL665 Sociocultural and Sociolinguistic Perspectives in Language Teaching (Spring, 2024)

Rationale

This essay on the Five Imperatives for Intercultural Communication is a strong representation of InTASC Standard 7: Planning for Instruction, because it demonstrates my ability to integrate deep sociocultural knowledge into instructional planning that is meaningful, ethical, and developmentally appropriate for language learners. Standard 7 emphasizes that teachers must draw on their understanding of subject matter, curriculum design, interdisciplinary connections, and instructional methods to plan learning experiences that support every learner. In this assignment, I worked to integrate theoretical perspectives from Jackson (2020) with real-world implications for how students experience language, identity, power, and global citizenship. Key elements that help create meaningful and well-structured instructional plans for teaching world languages.

By examining closely the major factors that shape communication across cultures, including global citizenship, worldwide interconnectedness, new technologies, population changes, and ethical concerns, I have come to understand how intercultural competence is no longer an optional element of language teaching, but an essential, future-oriented learning goal. This academic exploration directly informs how I plan lessons that move beyond vocabulary and grammar to cultivate empathy, perspective-taking, and critical cultural awareness intentionally. Jackson (2020) argues that intercultural competence involves developing the ability to understand and connect cultural behaviors while becoming aware of the biases and assumptions shaped by one's own cultural viewpoint. This insight helps me design instruction that fosters reflective thinking and expands students' understanding of themselves and others.

This assignment also strengthened my ability to plan instruction that is culturally sustaining. By analyzing examples of technological influence, cultural bias, and ethical dilemmas in intercultural interactions, I gained clarity about the need to prepare students to navigate diverse communicative contexts responsibly. These insights guide me in selecting authentic materials, designing reflective tasks, and preparing discussions that encourage students to question stereotypes, appreciate linguistic diversity, and develop culturally ethical communication habits.

Furthermore, the essay helps me plan instruction that supports students' 21st-century global competencies. Understanding how global mobility, digital communication, and shifting demographics reshape intercultural interactions enables me to create learning experiences that prepare students to succeed in multicultural communities. By grounding instruction in research, such as Jackson's (2020) arguments on globalization and Ryan's (2006, as cited in Jackson, 2020) insights about language dominance, I ensure that my curriculum meets both academic standards and real-world needs.

For these reasons, the Intercultural Communication 5 Imperatives Essay is a strong demonstration of InTASC Standard 7, showing how I intentionally connect sociocultural research with instructional planning that is globally relevant, culturally responsive, and deeply meaningful for my students.

Artifact #2: WL561 Textbook Review

Course: WL561 Teaching Languages (Fall, 2023)

Rationale

The textbook evaluation of Sonrisas Spanish demonstrates my ability to apply research-based criteria, pedagogical content knowledge, and cross-disciplinary perspectives to make informed instructional decisions, reflecting the core expectations of InTASC Standard 7: Planning for Instruction. This standard emphasizes that effective teachers "draw upon knowledge of content areas, curriculum, cross-disciplinary skills, and pedagogy to plan learning experiences that support every learner", a principle that guided the analysis throughout this activity.

Using the evaluation criteria adapted from Shrum and Glisan (2016), I critically examined how Sonrisas Spanish supports novice-level Spanish learners through communication-focused, developmentally appropriate instruction. The textbook's foundation in methodologies such as Total Physical Response, the Natural Approach, and the Waldorf World Language Approach (Hampshire & Lindner, 2021) allowed me to evaluate how research-supported practices are embedded in curricular materials and how they align with ACTFL proficiency targets for young learners. By conducting this structured evaluation, I demonstrated the ability to assess whether instructional resources reflect best practices in second language acquisition and whether they provide the scaffolds needed to support diverse learners in my K–4 classroom.

This activity also strengthened my capacity to plan learning experiences that integrate linguistic, cultural, visual, and kinesthetic modalities. Because Sonrisas Spanish incorporates cultural exploration, multiple intelligences, music, visuals, and storytelling, evaluating the program required me to consider how cross-disciplinary components, such as arts integration, literacy development, and cultural studies, can enhance language instruction. The process allowed me to determine which textbook features promote equitable access, meaningful communication, and authentic cultural learning, all of which support the intentional and inclusive lesson planning outlined in InTASC Standard 7.

Additionally, my evaluation included an examination of potential limitations, such as the need for additional reinforcement activities and authentic communicative tasks. This reflective critique is a key aspect of Standard 7, which requires teachers to adapt materials to meet learner needs and to make informed instructional choices rather than relying on resources uncritically. Identifying areas for enhancement, such as expanding opportunities for real-world language use, positions me to plan more purposeful and culturally responsive lessons aligned to ACTFL's 5 Cs.

Overall, this textbook evaluation is a strong representation of InTASC Standard 7 because it showcases my ability to analyze curriculum materials through a research-informed lens, plan instruction that reflects SLA principles, and ensure that resources meaningfully support diverse elementary Spanish learners.
